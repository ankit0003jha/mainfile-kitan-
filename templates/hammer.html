TYPES OF HAMMERS

MALLET

A mallet is a kind of hammer, often made of rubber or sometimes wood, that is smaller than a maul or beetle, and usually has a relatively large head.

CLAW HAMMER

A claw hammer is a tool primarily used for driving nails into, or pulling nails from, some other object. Generally, a claw hammer is associated with woodworking.

FRAMING HAMMER

Framing hammers, used for framing wooden houses, are heavy duty rip hammers with a straight claw. The head will be made of steel and the handle of wood.

SLEDGE HAMMER

A sledgehammer is a tool with a large, flat, often metal head, attached to a long handle. The long handle combined with a heavy head allows the sledgehammer to gather momentum during a swing

TACK HAMMER

An upholstery hammer (also called a tack hammer) is a lightweight hammer used for securing upholstery fabric to furniture frames using small nails. face has a split surface to make its magnetic hold stronger.

CLUB HAMMER

This is used for hitting cold chisels to break masonry or simply to bash things up like a tiny sledge hammer. They are usually double faced and have a wood or resin handle.

BALL-PEEN HAMMER

A ball-peen or ball pein hammer, also known as a machinist's hammer, is a type of peening hammer used in metalworking. It has two heads, one flat and the other, called the peen, rounded.
