TYPES OF PLANE

SCRUB PLANE

Originally designed for ripping work, this plane removes material quickly, allowing you to switch to your bench planes once an oversized board is trimmed down to size.

SHOULDER PLANE

Shoulder planes closely resemble bullnose planes in design, but measure up to eight inches in length. Much like a bullnose plane, they’re great for detail work.

BLOCK PLANE

Generally considered the DIY go-to for its low cost and compact size, a good block plane can work wonders. They’re designed for cutting end grain, shaping, and chamfering, and can be used one-handed.

JACK PLANE

A jack plane is a general-purpose wood-working bench plane, used for dressing timber down to the correct size in preparation for truing edge jointing. It is usually the first plane used on rough stock,

KANNA(japanese-plane)

The pinnacle of carpentry, the fine art of Japanese woodcraft relies heavily on the kanna. These are very simplistic hand planes, consisting only of a Japanese oak body, blade, and chip breaker pin.
